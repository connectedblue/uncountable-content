<p>A large delegation from the National Lottery visited the <a href="https://www.cotswoldcanalsconnected.org/">Cotswold Canals Connected</a> project today to review progress on different aspects.  The <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a> wanted to showcase the riverfly monitoring work that has taken place over the past few years.</p>

<p>The overall score was 6 &#8211; not too bad for this site, but still a shockingly low number of gammarus.  Just 8 in the whole tray.  After the formal samples, we took a couple of buckets back to the lawn in front of St Cyr&#8217;s church  ready for the lottery visitors.</p>



<p>We had just 15 minutes for the whole thing. Pete gave an introduction to the Wildlife Trust and their role in the project and setting up the riverfly monitoring and then handed over to me to talk about the activities we&#8217;ve done. This was not only the formal monthly citizen science surveys, but also work we&#8217;ve done with <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/07/primary-school-riverfly/" data-type="post" data-id="6221">primary</a> and <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/05/riverfly-with-biology-students/" data-type="post" data-id="6082">secondary</a> schools to engage the wider community in the activity.</p>



<p>They then had a short period to look more closely at the samples and ask more questions before they went off to their next activity.  Overall we felt the visit was a success and a good showcase of what we have been doing.</p>



<p>In the evening, we held the regular monthly <a href="https://dursleygreen.org.uk/dursley-green-drinks/">Dursley Green Drinks</a> which was quite well attended, including new people.  There was lots of good discussion, including quite a lot on forthcoming climate awareness events which would be good to coordinate more closely across the various groups in the town.</p>
