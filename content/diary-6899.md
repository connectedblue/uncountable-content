<p>One of my favourite parts of the Fromebridge reserve is also the least visited.  A <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/series/orchard-pond/" data-type="post_tag" data-id="62">small pond</a> in the far north west corner, inaccessible except by cutting through vegetation.  We go there once a year with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a> to maintain it, allow light back in through the canopy and cut back encroaching vegetation.</p>

<p>I worked with Caroline on the east side of the pond, where there wasn&#8217;t ready access around the edge. It was overgrown with bramble and also an old barb wire fence tangled up in it that we also removed.</p>



<p>We managed to make pretty good progress by tea break.  We had largely cleared the ground area, removed the barb and it was possible to pass both sides of the pond.  We created a new dead hedge pile underneath a beautiful old willow which has fallen over away from the pond, but still very much alive.  </p>



<p>By lunchtime we had the whole area pretty much done.  The north bank is perfectly positioned to capture the sun, so we&#8217;ll try seeding it with a neutral wildflower and grassland mix.  So we raked off the remaining vegetation to create a bare earth slope.</p>



<p>We&#8217;ll return again in the summer when the pond is dry to remove the remaining willow trunks in the middle of the pond. </p>
