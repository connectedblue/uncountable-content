<p>Returned again to another a long running habitat management site with <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a>.  I was last here <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2024/11/third-year-restoration/" data-type="post" data-id="3919">a year ago</a> and it was good to see the previous areas of restoration still in good condition as a result of the grazing regime.</p>



<p>It took a little while to get going.  Chris and I decided to tackle a bramble patch on a south facing slope, but we both had left our PPE back in the cars.  By the time we had everything together, we managed about 10 minutes of cutting before the tea break.</p>

<p>The patch was well above head height and also growing into overhanging branches.  So we sliced out &#8220;cubes&#8221; of bramble piece by piece while the other raked.</p>



<p>By lunchtime we had the main scrub removed, and just the arisings to clear away to a nearby hollow.</p>

<p>After clearing the main area, we could also pollard some of the overhanging branches which will reduce leaf shading.  And finally we raked quite hard down to soil to remove as much nutrient as possible.</p>



<p>The team on the other side of the copse were working on removing trees on the edge of the grassland and had constructed pretty much the biggest habitat pile we&#8217;ve ever had.</p>



<p>In the evening, I went down to Kingshill House to host the <a href="https://dursleygreen.org.uk/dursley-green-drinks/">Dursley Green Drinks</a>.  A smaller crowd today, but a couple of new faces and some good conversations in the groups.</p>
