<p>Returning to a long running site with <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a> which has fly orchids and the Duke of Burgundy butterfly.  The main task today was to clear hazel on either side of the top track to expose connecting strips of grassland with other parts of the site.</p>

<p>After the break, I took over the brush cutting while Chris raked behind.  The idea in the grassland is to create wide short path among the longer grass which allows cowslips to thrive that the Duke of Burgundy needs to feed.    We extended previously cut paths further into the grassland.  </p>



<p>After lunch it was back to the main track and final sprint to keep up with Tom and the chainsaw.  By the end of the task the transformation to the track was incredible and good quality grassland should develop over the next couple of years.</p>



<p>In the evening we held the first <a href="https://dursleygreen.org.uk/dursley-green-drinks/">Dursley Green Drinks</a> of 2026, also for the first time in the <a href="https://www.chantrycentre.org/">Chantry Centre</a>.  The weather was foul &#8211; cold, heavy rain and rivers of rainwater sloshing down Long Street.  It would be hardy souls who made it out tonight.</p>



<p>The turnout was extraordinary.  Twenty people, several of whom were attending for the first time.  And so many diverse projects from community energy, National Nature day for Dursley, setting up a local CAN, Freecycle projects, Stinchcombe Hill grazing, Food sustainability and many others.  A huge amount of talent in the room, and this is exactly why we set up green drinks &#8211; loads of animated conversations and connections being made across the room.</p>
