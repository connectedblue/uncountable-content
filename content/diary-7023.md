<p>Water vole displacement activity continued this week with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a>.  The translocation will happen over the weekend and next week, so we needed to get the release pens ready.</p>



<p>I went over to the release area with Imogen and Suzi.  The basic idea was to have the nose of the pen in the waterline, and the lid at the back for easy access.  This will allow the licensed ecologist a chance to transfer the translocated vole safely into the pen, from where they can make their own way out.</p>

<p>So we re-assessed and realised we needed to dig a spade width trench in a rectangle to seat it properly.</p>

<p>We finished the first one by tea break.  The second one wouldn&#8217;t really fit where we had agreed with Pete &#8211; the slope was too steep.  So we found a more suitable spot closer to the canal.</p>



<p>After both were secured in place, we put in grass bedding from the habitat pile, along with a couple of saucers of water.</p>



<p>The rest of the team had finished the work around the oil pipeline, so we ended up leaving site around half an hour earlier than expected.</p>
