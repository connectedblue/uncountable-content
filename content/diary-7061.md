<p>Hard to believe it&#8217;s already April, and time for the next round of riverfly counts after we got started <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2026/03/a-riverfly-lottery/" data-type="post" data-id="7018">with a bang</a> last month.   Met up early with Graham to walk over to our regular site.</p>



<p>The levels had not changed very much since last time &#8211; still a little high and flowing fast, but slightly less than last time.  Graham went in the water this time and I timed on the bank.</p>



<p>As usual, the first glance at the bucket didn&#8217;t reveal very much, but it soon became apparent when we got it into the sample tray that there was a lot going on.</p>



<p>Still a couple of mayfly, although nowhere near the numbers last month.  Season must be almost over.  We did find a mayfly skin where the larvae had pupated which was interesting.</p>



<p>The big find was a large number of blue winged olives.  Around 24.  I find these very difficult to distinguish from stone clingers sometimes and there were a few we looked at very closely.  But in the end, we were confident they were all blue wing.</p>



<p>Overall a score of 7, which is pretty encouraging for this site.</p>
