<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Went early to the Ocean to meet with Graham for our regular riverfly site monitor.  The levels were down quite a bit since last month, and the flow was nowhere near as strong.  So we were able to sample in most parts where we normally go.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The sample looked immediately promising on tipping the net into the bucket &#8211; lots of things swimming about.  And within a couple of minutes we already had mayfly, cased and caseless caddis.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The two species I struggle with are blue-winged olives and stone clingers &#8211; they look very similar except the stone clingers are a bit squatter and the gills are on the side.  Usually they are blue-winged though.  The Blue-iwinged also have a rocking horse motion.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">But today we had loads of them literally clinging to stones in the sample tray.  So it made ID much easier because once you have a couple of good examples, it&#8217;s easier to classify new ones.  But either way, we had over ten of each.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">So overall, the score for the site was 11 &#8211; easily the largest we have ever recorded there.  So things looking good for river health going into the summer.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The room looks incredible.  It&#8217;s a very polished and professional escape room and that was the first time I&#8217;d seen it with everything in.  I can&#8217;t show a photo because we don&#8217;t want the room secrets all over the web.  The first two groups of players were from <a href="https://www.the-dods.com/">the DODS theatre group</a>, who are based at the Chantry.  </p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The game takes around an hour, and I hung around upstairs to hear the first pieces of feedback.  It&#8217;s certainly interesting to see how players play the game in a very different way to the sequence you intended, so we may have to make tweaks to the format after a series of pilot runs over the next few weeks.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I was pleased to see that the <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2026/04/installing-the-escape-room-puzzle/" data-type="post" data-id="7117">tablet and traffic light</a> held up during the game and performed as expected.  It was an interesting community project to be involved with and hopefully this will be a fixture in the town for many years to come.</p>
