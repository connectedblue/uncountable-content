<p>Last Orchard work party of 2025 with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a> this morning.  Pete reflected on the work we&#8217;ve done and how fabulous the orchard is looking.  We were at the southern corner today, preparing an area for some hedge laying on the boundary.</p>

<p>There was quite a bit of scrub to remove before we could even access the hedge line.  Most of the team started work on that task, while Matt and I cleared the final bits of willow from the area of scrub we&#8217;d been <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/11/tidying-the-orchard-corner/" data-type="post" data-id="6669">removing recently</a>.</p>



<p>After a while, I got the fire going as we were generating quite a bit of material.  The willow was dry, so it didn&#8217;t take too long for the fire to heat up nicely.</p>

<p>We stopped for a break around 11.30am and Pete had prepared some mulled cider on a camping stove.</p>



<p>It was a beautifully sunny morning and nice to chat with everyone over cake as well.</p>
</div></div>

</div></div>


<p>By this time the hedge team had made great progress clearing a pathway towards the hedge to be laid.  We discovered a stock proof fence there, and also a drainage ditch in between the two lines of older trees.  There was also quite a bit of bramble that needs brush cutting out from the trees before we can start.  </p>



<p>So a few problems to solve at the next work party, but for the first time we can see the scope of what needs doing to create the hedge.  By the time we finished the fire had reduced down to a manageable size which could be left to burn out on its own.</p>
