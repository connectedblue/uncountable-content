<p>We planted around 15 younger trees over the last few years and each has a stock proof barrier, a stake and some deer/rabbit guards.</p>



<p>The idea today was to get in the middle and take apart any guards and fixings, do some weeding and then see what needs to be put back.</p>

<p>The staking approach is a bit of an art more than science.  We would give the tree a gentle shake and make sure it didn&#8217;t catch or touch the deer guard.  And then we&#8217;d see if the ground moved also with the tree.  If it did, then the stake still needs to be in place as the tree roots have not yet established firmly enough.</p>



<p>I removed some plastic cable ties from some and replaced with metal wire which can be unpicked more easily in future than the single use cable tie.  Also, a few of the rabbit guards had become very brittle and ineffective so I removed them.</p>



<p>There were only a few of us but we managed to get through all the trees during the session, with a bit of time at the end to cut dock seed heads off to try and prevent them spreading further.</p>



<p>Late afternoon, Charlie texted me with a problem configuring the <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2026/04/installing-the-escape-room-puzzle/" data-type="post" data-id="7117">escape room application</a>.  It&#8217;s the inaugural test tomorrow with a local group, so we need to get it right.  It turned out there were a couple of bugs in the app, so I worked on a new release to iron them out.  I&#8217;ll wander down to the Chantry tomorrow to help get everything ready for the first run.</p>
