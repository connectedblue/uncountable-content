<p>Tasks continue at Fromebridge with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a>.  Today we begun preparations on the famous oil pipeline which passes over the canal by Occupation Bridge.</p>



<p>Famous because the work to bury it under the canal has been repeatedly delayed for years.  And famous because it&#8217;s <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H-I6drGa2pk">been on YouTube</a>.   However, before the work can commence in May, we have to make sure that no water voles will be within 18 meters of the pipeline (a legal requirement for construction near endangered species).</p>

<p>Andrew and I had the brush cutters, so we started on the opposite bank, while the rest of the team removed the bramble around the pipeline itself by hand.  The vegetation wasn&#8217;t all that long, but the bank was steep so careful cutting was required so I wouldn&#8217;t end up in the canal.</p>



<p>We finished that side in time for tea break, and delicious cake and biscuits from Susie and Suzi.  We then tackled the near side while the others cleared the arisings on the other side.</p>



<p>I left early today as I was meeting Georgie in Dursley Market Place to plan the layout for <a href="https://offbeatcollective.org.uk/">Offbeat Dursley</a> in June.  We got the keys to the stores and pulled all the tables out into the Market Place to see where all the parts of the event would take place.  </p>



<p>Was definitely worth doing a dry run like this &#8211; much easier to visualise than trying to do on paper.</p>
