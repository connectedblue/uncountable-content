We&#8217;ve worked previously on clearing the grassland of hawthorn scrub, so we continued the same technique on the adjacent section.</p>

<p>We decided to only start brush cutting after the mid morning break because people were working in the area we wanted to clear.  So I helped with some scrub dragging and had a go with tree popping some of the hawthorn.  I was getting a success rate of about 1 in 3 &#8211; most of them were multi stemmed and didn&#8217;t pull, or too thin  and snapped.</p>



<p>Once we got going though, Chris and I strimmed the main area pretty quickly ready for the rakers after lunch.  I took the brush cutter over to the other side of the slope while Karen, Ruth and John did a very efficient job of removing all the cut grass, exposing some fine ant hills.</p>
