<p>I&#8217;ve been looking forward to this day for the best part of a year now.  Vicky had organised a group trip to the <a href="https://gloucestershireefw.co.uk/">Gloucestershire Energy from Waste</a> facility at Javelin Park.</p>



<p>This impressive facility takes 92% of household waste from Gloucestershire (including kerbside pickups, the &#8220;non-recyclables&#8221; at recycling centres and other sources) and performs a recovery process to extract energy and other products.  This means that there is now no new landfill sites in the county and additional money can be raised from the extracted products.</p>



<p>The facility is licensed to process 190,000 tons of waste annually, of which 130,000 tonnes comes from the council collected household waste.  The balance is from commercial contracts.</p>

<p>We started off with a very interesting presentation and discussion of how the whole process works.</p>



<p>Essentially there is waste in and metals, building aggregate and electricity are extracted. Cleaned steam is released through the chimney (this is pure steam plus <a href="https://gloucestershireefw.co.uk/emissions-monitoring">permissible levels of pollutant</a> monitored by the EA).</p>

<p>There is then a small amount of hazardous waste that can&#8217;t be used as product and this is then put into landfill at specialist facilities.  The electricity produced is a constant 16.5MW which is enough to power 25,000 homes.  50% of this electricity can be classified as renewable under the guidelines.</p>



<p>We then had a tour around the plant to see the various processes in action. So we saw the lorries tipping the raw waste into a giant pit, claws lifting it out into a sloping grate where it slides down at 1100C, the giant burning furnace, the electrity generator and the waste gas cleaning process (essentially giant socks that act like a hoover bag).</p>



<p>The whole tour was fascinating and I feel much better informed about how waste is managed in the county, and how much can be saved by not creating landfill.  </p>
