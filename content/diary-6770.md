<p>Second work party of the winter season for Uley Trees and Meadows, and the first in the new playing field project.  Eight of us gathered in the pavilion car park for a briefing from Vicky about the tree planting task.</p>



<p>We had three wild cherries, an oak and a number of hawthorn whips to plant around the edge.  The larger standards will be memorial trees to those villagers who died during the war.</p>

<p>By the time of the break all the trees were in the ground.  So we could spend the remaining time clearing the cut grass from the corner patch.  Space is limited in the playfield for clippings, so we used a tarpaulin to drag them away to a hidden corner on the other side of the field.  </p>



<p>We had some discussions about how big an area to restore.  The long term answer to this depends very much on the ability to sustain human cutting and raking of the area.  This is because the area is neither grazed or cut for hay.</p>



<p>So we&#8217;ll keep it to a small area for now and see how they develop.  Over the longer term, it may be feasible to attach a mower with grass collector onto the back of a tractor to allow a longer wildflower strip in future.</p>
