<p>We&#8217;ve done a lot of work to remove overhanging branches, so my goal today was to cut quite low in order to see if the grass will spread there next growing season.  Along the way I cut a few more final overhanging limbs.  Closer to the woodland edge there is a lot of leaf litter and ground ivy, so it will be a challenge for wildflowers to thrive there, but we&#8217;ll see what happens.</p>



<p>Meanwhile David was progressing nicely on the other side, and we now have clear ground up to the scrub boundary, again giving the maximum chance for the grassland to expand.</p>



<p>We managed to rake most of it off before we finished.  I&#8217;ll return on Tuesday to complete the last bit.  Then only one more session required this winter before the growing season starts in earnest.</p>
