<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The plan this morning was to give a Biology lesson to Year 12 students at <a href="https://rednockschool.org.uk/">Rednock School</a> on Riverfly.  We did the <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/05/riverfly-with-biology-students/" data-type="post" data-id="6082">same thing last yea</a>r, so we have a well practised routine which can be delivered within the double period allocated.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Before heading into the school, I went down to the site to check the vegetation and access for the students.  The usual way in to the site was completely impassable and I had no chance of clearing the path with just a pair of loppers.  So I investigated the alternate route which, while very steep, was much easier to access the river itself.<br><br>Tony and I then met with the students in the classroom first for an initial briefing, and then we headed down to the river.  They divided into four groups and after a quick demonstration they chose different parts of the river to do a kick and hand sample according to the methodology.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We then took the buckets back to the science lab and spent the second period doing ID of the species and recording the counts and scores found.  The students were all very engaged in the activity and were pretty good at identifying the different larvae once they got their eye in.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The overall scores for the site were pretty encouraging and were in line with what we have seen there during our formal recording.  After finishing the double period, Tony and I took the samples back to the river.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In the afternoon, I held the <a href="https://www.facebook.com/dursleycodeclub">Dursley Code Club</a> at the library.  We&#8217;re getting close to the end of term now and the attendance is starting to drop off.  But the regulars who came today continued to impress.  I reviewed the code of a youtube clone made by one of the Year 7s and it was very impressive.  Not just because it was featured rich and fully operational with all his friends, but mainly because he had hand coded it.  He uses AI for assistance, but the app itself has all the functionality added in manually by himself.   It was an astonishing complete project including security considerations.</p>
