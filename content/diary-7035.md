<p>Today was the last winter habitat management task of the season with <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a>, and it was my seventh year in a row restoring a grassland bank at Budge Hill.</p>



<p>The site has been grazed <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2024/12/budging-grass-at-budge-hill/" data-type="post" data-id="3981">since we were last here</a> and the visual difference was stunning.  The rough scrub on the east field which you can see in my diary entry from that day was replaced by grassland right up to the field boundary and and far as the eye could see. Very encouraging to see such good progress.</p>

<p>Sadly, the rampant hornbeam in the opposite field has encroached much further into the grassland and it&#8217;s very difficult to know what to do next.  A 10x10m area was marked out and cut low as an experiment.</p>



<p>A very pleasant lunch around the field gate finished off the season nicely, and will be the last time we&#8217;re together as a group until November.</p>



<p>In the evening, I went down to GL11 where a Sri Lankan meal was cooked for the community.  The food was absolutely delicious and excellent company made a very pleasant night.</p>
