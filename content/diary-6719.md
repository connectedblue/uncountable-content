Instead of meeting in a restaurant for a meal, we decided to have a trip to the <a href="https://www.wwt.org.uk/wetland-centres/slimbridge">wetlands at Slimbridge</a> and take a festive picnic.</p>

<p>My bird ID skills are non-existent, so I was content with just trying to figure out how many different species I could see without worrying about what they were.</p>



<p>But with so much expertise in the group, it was good to take a look at some of the rarer birds that could be spotted.  There was also a very helpful volunteer guide who could give information about what was visiting this time of year.</p>



<p>We stopped for lunch in a nice room with chairs that overlooked the pond.  We&#8217;d all brought various festive mixes which we shared while we chatted and caught up on news.</p>



<p>Afterwards, we headed into the main reserve to some of the remote hides on the other side of the visitor attractions.  A marsh harrier was spotted in and out of some areas, but then disappeared into some woodland.</p>



<p>We stayed there until well after 3pm, when the light was being to fade and people had other appointments.  So it turned out to be a much more memorable christmas do than the usual.</p>
