<p>Returned with <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a> to another favourite and lovely site at the top of Cleeve Hill.  The last few times I was here, we focused on <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/01/restoring-quarry-grassland/" data-type="post" data-id="4103">restoring a quarry face</a>, but today we were in another part of the site, which was steep and covered with dips and hollows from past quarry activity.</p>



<p>The land is grazed by cattle and ponies and extremely well managed.  Our task today was to remove encroaching thorns and bramble from parts of the grassy slopes. While the rest of the team got on with scrub bashing, Chris and I took the brush cutters to the far end of the site to make a start there.</p>

<p>I then joined Chris at the far end of the site in a south facing slope with superb views over Cheltenham.  A lot of the hollows here were filled with bramble so this was a more straightforward removal task.  With the added bonus of wild clematis mixed in to trip us up.</p>



<p>There was a wild storm last night, but today was superb weather &#8211; bright blue sky and pretty mild. Cerian had made some very tasty mince pies and welsh cakes to have with the morning coffee in the sun.</p>



<p>Each year we pick one site to visit in June to survey and see the results of our efforts, and this one was suggested and locked in.  There&#8217;s plenty of areas of interest, so look forward to visiting again in the warmer months.</p>
