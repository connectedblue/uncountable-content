<p>After the last few weeks diversions at <a href="https://wildacres.org.uk/">Wild Acres</a> getting ready for the developing roots day, I got the chance to return to the other end of the meadow to continue the hazel coppicing.</p>



<p>There are quite a few mature trees in that corner which can be selectively managed to stimulate future product growth, remove shading from the meadow and clear a path by the brook to create a connecting circular walk around the site.</p>

<p>This will enable visitors to explore the whole perimeter of the site along the brook and then down by the new hedge.  There&#8217;s quite a bit of work still to do &#8211; there&#8217;s rubbish in the brook itself which needs removing and some old fencing in the undergrowth.  There&#8217;s also a couple of spidery willow that will need chainsaw removal.</p>



<p>I went over the plan with Martin, who agreed to the work.  I&#8217;ll put it down in the management plan, and set about doing it over the next few weeks.  There&#8217;s quite a lot to do, so I&#8217;ll see if I can recruit some friends from other projects who might want to help.</p>



<p>In the afternoon, I went down to the library for the <a href="https://www.facebook.com/dursleycodeclub">Dursley Code Club</a>.  Unfortunately, the major problems from yesterday were still present and we weren&#8217;t able to reliably login to the coding sites.  So the children had an hour playing games instead, which they seemed super happy about.  Hopefully I can get their interest back to coding next week.</p>
