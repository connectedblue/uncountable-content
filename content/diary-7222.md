<p class="wp-block-paragraph">After months of planning, it&#8217;s the last day to get everything ready for <a href="https://offbeatcollective.org.uk/">Offbeat Dursley</a>.  I met with Georgie at the Market House to pick up the keys and get the upstairs ready.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The weather forecast was looking very dicey for tomorrow, so we needed to make a few contingencies, especially for the choir.  I had a few calls with the choir team to see exactly how many would be turning up and what they had in the way of equipment.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There was a load of rather unsightly floor cleaning equipment and other random materials in the corner of the room, so I stacked them in the small kitchen which we wouldn&#8217;t be using.  I don&#8217;t think they had been moved for years, as they were thick with dust behind.  A quick sweep and we suddenly had a lot more room and pleasant environment to look at.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We opened the upstairs and set up the tables for the craft areas and also a makeshift stage with audience chairs.  Georgie had made some fabulous window banners which we hung from the curtain rails.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We could then leave the remaining gear in the market house to give a head start for tomorrow.</p>
