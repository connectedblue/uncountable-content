<p>Now that <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/series/winter-habitat-management-2025-26/" data-type="post_tag" data-id="60">grassland winter habitat management</a> is over, I was free to spend my first Wednesday this year with <a href="https://www.stroudvalleysproject.org/">Stroud Valleys Project</a>.  New site for me also, where they have spent a couple of years planting a huge amount of hedgerow.</p>



<p>It was good to see Ruth again, and catch up with others I hadn&#8217;t seen in a while.  There were also quite a few new people who had joined the group over winter, which is always great to see.</p>

<p>There were quite a few of us, but some logistic challenges as the pile of mulch was a good 300m away from where it needed to be.  And with only three wheelbarrows, it would take quite a while to shift.</p>



<p>So we press ganged the truck into service by filling the flat bed and driving the mulch down the track where it needed to be.  After around 3 or 4 trips, the pile was exhausted  so there was a longer drive back to the farm to pick up more.</p>



<p>The weather up until lunchtime was very pleasant.  Warm, dry but very windy in this exposed part of the Severn Vale.  That quickly turned during lunch as the heavens opened for the remainder of the session.</p>



<p>A few hardy souls went on the back of the truck to refill the truck with mulch.  They must have been thoroughly soaked through.  The rest of us stayed in a barn shelter until they returned.  A couple more refills pretty much finished the line and we could get back to the cars and dry off.</p>



<p>In the evening, we hosted the regular <a href="https://dursleygreen.org.uk/dursley-green-drinks/">Dursley Green Drinks</a> at the Chantry.  Very well attended, with four new faces who hadn&#8217;t been before.  Some good conversations about the various projects going on in town at the moment. </p>
