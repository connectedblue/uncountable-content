<p>This morning it was showing very promising signs, even this early in the season.  There were already substantial patches of primerose in the southern end, which was covered last year.  But second patches had appeared at the northern end next to the dead hedge which was previously covered by thick bramble.  </p>



<p>Also very encouraging was seeing grass sprout on both the eastern scrub line and the western woodland edge. We have had a couple of attempts over the last two winters to cut away the bramble and rake away nutrient. Early days, but seems to be paying off with an expansion of the grassland area.</p>



<p>Also out was the ribwort plantain and a field scabious/knapweed (hard to tell which from the small leaves).  Over in the corner, the triangle of dog&#8217;s mercury was springing up nicely.  </p>



<p>All in all, very promising for the forthcoming growing season.   </p>
