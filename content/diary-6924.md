<p>A beautiful morning for the monthly Fromebridge orchard work party with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a>. Given the heavy rain of the last weeks, the ground was too water logged for the truck, so we had to carry the tools across to the top of the orchard.</p>



<p>There were a few tasks to progress for the team, but I chose to complete the last tree guard which I had started, but <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/06/last-orchard-guard/" data-type="post" data-id="6160">abandoned last summer</a>.  The ground back then was impossibly firm to drive in the posts &#8211; something that wouldn&#8217;t be an issue today.</p>

<p>I worked with Nick and Suzi to first of all drive the posts to the correct depth.  They had been slightly knocked about by cattle, but with some careful spirit level work, we could then them aligned.</p>



<p>It took hardly any effort to drive them in &#8211; in fact we were a bit too hammer happy and drove them 5cm deeper than intended.</p>



<p>It was then a matter of attaching the rails in a way that didn&#8217;t look too wonky given the underlying orchard slope.</p>

<p>After a break, I carried on with sawing off the ends.  We had used half moon rails here instead of the rectangular ones elsewhere in the orchard which made lining up the ends much more tricky.</p>



<p>For some reason, the wood saw pinched so tightly half way down it became impossible to saw.  So I used a combination of silkie and wood saw to complete the job.</p>



<p>After a quick tidy up of the site, we were ready to transport the tools back to the truck just before 1pm.</p>
