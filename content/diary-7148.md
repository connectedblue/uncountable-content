<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The annual survey season with <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a> kicks off today with a training session and refresher on methodology.  Although I don&#8217;t take part in many surveys, I do the app and data analysis.  So today I was on hand to explain how the app works for new users and also get any usability issues before the formal surveys start in earnest.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We were in a site I&#8217;d not been to before in Little Barrington.  It was a really stunning species rich bank and a great spot for training in indicator species.  I started out by going through the app with everyone.  This always seems quite long winded, as there are a few odd little things you have to do to record correctly, but once you&#8217;ve done it a couple of times it makes more sense.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">This is the third year of using the app, and we did spot a few scenarios where data could be lost, which I hadn&#8217;t spotted before.  I&#8217;ll update the app over the weekend to ensure it&#8217;s all working well for next week.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I did a quadrat with Sheila and Jane and we found quite a few different species such a mouse ear chickweed and spring wort (which I&#8217;ve never heard before).  Rough hawkbit was also present which could be identified by inspecting the forked hairs with the hand lense on the edge of the leaf.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">After a coffee break, we headed over to the village green which was incredibly species rich.  Early marsh orchid was in flower there, as well as  Star of Bethlehem.  There was also yellow rattle which is the first I&#8217;ve seen this season.</p>
