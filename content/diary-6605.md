<p>A glorious morning at Fromebridge reserve with <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a>.  A big team turned out, including Ellie, our volunteer coordinator who I&#8217;ve not seen for ages.</p>



<p>The first part of the morning Barry, Matt and I worked on a repair to the stock proof fence behind the hedge we planted a couple of years ago.  The tops of the hedge plants have definitely been nibbled which stunts their growth.  There were cows in the field so we couldn&#8217;t take it down and reset &#8211; we needed to do in place to keep cattle in.</p>



<p>We had six posts and found the weakest ones for removal and replacement. The posts we use now were quite a bit taller so we can place the top row of barb a lot higher. The technique was similar for each &#8211; unstaple the old post from the netting and barb, ram a new post in and re-tension the wires.</p>

<p>The task today was to really knock back a dense patch of scrub between the hedge line and the grassland.  This will enable the hedge to be restored properly and will also create quite a significant amount of new grassland area.</p>



<p>The team had already cleared a huge amount when we arrived.  They took the brush cutter to the easier stuff, but then the tangle of bramble and trees meant it needed to be done by hand.  There was also a hawthorn which had been historically coppiced so we did that too.</p>



<p>The bulk of the brash was placed over the stock fence out of the way of the grassland and the larger branches were kept to make a dead hedge in the gaps now revealed in the main hedgeline.  There will be future tasks to lay this hedge and plant new whips in the gaps to make a structured boundary that also is also a multiple age habitat.</p>



<p>By 1pm, the weather turned very chilly and grey all of a sudden.  The rain held off though until we finished up around 2pm.</p>
