<p>I didn&#8217;t really have a route in mind when setting out, but headed out towards some favourite tracks.  Walking in remote woods, I was surprised to see such a carpet of bluebells this early in the year.</p>



<p>The wood anemone was also out so the woods were looking pretty fine.  After about an hour&#8217;s walking, I had finalised a route in mind which would then take another hour.</p>



<p>The weather was very mild, and I noticed that the ground is very dry. Despite the heavy winter rains, there&#8217;s not been any for a while and it&#8217;s already starting to show in hard ground.</p>

<p>I finally ended up on Uley Bury and decided to do a full circuit which is about a mile.  I stopped to admire the habitat management that had taken place over the winter and then heard the unmistakable song of the skylark.  There&#8217;s plenty nesting in the middle of the bury and there was a cacophony of noise on the southern side.</p>



<p>In the afternoon, I finally got around to cutting the grass in the back garden ready for the growing season.  Hopefully the wildflowers will now get a chance to compete with the grasses over the next month.</p>
