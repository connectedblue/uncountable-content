<p class="wp-block-paragraph">At this stage in the season, there is way too much to pull by hand.  Cutting them now means they won&#8217;t go to seed (and make the problem worse), but of course, being perennial plants means they will come back next year.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The worst areas were quite distinctive because the spear thistle grows tall (and the creeping thistle seems to keep up with it).  So my approach was just to move quickly through those patches, cutting down at the root.  We won&#8217;t bother raking up as the goal is preventing seed dispersal rather than reducing nutrient.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The cows were very curious and gathered round me for the first 15 minutes.  They didn&#8217;t come any closer though, perhaps fearing the very loud machine I was carrying.  I did notice they started chomping in between where I had cut, so that should help spread the grass where the patches were.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">With this hot weather, the thistles will wilt quite quickly and I&#8217;m quite interested to see whether they then become tasty for the cows to eat up.  We&#8217;ll know next time we return and check if those piles of thistle are still there.<br></p>
