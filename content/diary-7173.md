<p>My second outing with the box wood volunteer group to finish off the bridge work we had started a <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2026/04/bridge-building-in-box/" data-type="post" data-id="7119">couple of weeks ago</a>.</p>



<p>The railway sleepers had bedded in nicely over the intervening weeks and the surfaces leading up to it on each side had compacted with use and weather.  The task today was to install chicken wire to make the surface non-slip, and also to dig out some access steps.</p>



<p>The width of wire was just slightly shorter than the five planks of bridge, and the main challenge was just to get the right amount of tension and stretch to secure it with the staples.  We started out by digging enough soil out the ends to bury the wire underneath, and then placing the staples in the right position to catch the tension.</p>



<p>It was a fiddly job best done with three people to secure everything.  Meanwhile the rest of the team worked on leveling and digging the access leading to the bridge on one side.</p>



<p>There&#8217;ll need to be some wood edging applied to the dug steps, but otherwise the project is now complete and functioning.</p>



<p>We had spare wire so went down further into the wood where a previous bridge installed 8 years ago needed a renewal of the chicken wire.  A shorter task today, but finished just in time before the rain came down.</p>



<p>In the evening, I went down to the Old Spot to meet with David and Harri to progress discussions about forming Digital Dursley.  We are agreed in principle that we should formalise the group, and I have a couple of actions to follow through in the next fortnight to see if we can set up a fiscal host to support the group.</p>
