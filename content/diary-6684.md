So today at Fromebridge with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a> was a good opportunity to change that.</p>

<p>The reserve boundary with the Greene King field has a couple of old oaks, a few elder and a quite a few very mature hawthorn.</p>



<p>The idea was to lay a hedge to create a rich habitat at the base of the hedge, as well as a stock proof boundary in future years.</p>

<p>We had a variety of tools &#8211; bill hook, felling axe and silkies.  The main challenge was selecting which stem to do next and clearing a path to it.</p>



<p>The thinner stems were pretty easy to do with the bill hook &#8211; just a few chops really and they could then be pulled horizontally to the ground.</p>
</div></div>

</div></div>


<p>It looks a destructive technique, but as long as you leave a live &#8220;hinge&#8221; attached at the base of the tree, the laid hedge will spring into life with new shoots next year.  The larger stems took quite a while to chop &#8211; the pros would be doing those with a mini chainsaw.</p>



<p>There were probably around 12 main stems to lay which we managed to do by lunch, and the other team completed theirs at around the same time.  So altogether about 10-15 meters of hedge laid.  Not the fastest, but most of us were new to it and picking up the technique.  </p>



<p>After lunch we stacked the remaining brash into the hedge, and next time we&#8217;ll stake it with hazel to keep it down.</p>
