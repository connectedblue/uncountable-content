<p>Another new site for me today with <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a> up at Ravensgate Common above Cheltenham. This has been seeded by the project before, but the task today was to remove encroaching scrub, principally hawthorn, blackthorn and gorse.</p>



<p>It was an extremely foggy start &#8211; you couldn&#8217;t see the amazing views until around mid morning.  There was also a biting wind at the top of the hill so we needed to get moving quickly.</p>

<p>The brush cutting turned out to be fairly minimal in the end.  The higher you went up, the cutting blade just hovered above the grass.  Anna and David cleared the few trees and I found a reasonable nearby place to stack a dead hedge.  </p>



<p>There were some 3 to 4 foot high blackthorns along the scrub edge, but we decided to keep those for nesting birds and the quality of grassland inside them was poor anyway.  So in the end I just cut a meter back into it to stop it encroaching further and expose a bit more grass.</p>



<p>Hopefully the net result will encourage the cattle up the slope to graze it down during next season.</p>



<p>After lunch we all went over to the seeded slope where the tree popping team had been working.  It was all hands on deck to get the last bit of scrub removed and dragged/carried/rolled to a dead hedge at the bottom.</p>



<p>Very interesting site and will be good to return to to develop further in future years.</p>
