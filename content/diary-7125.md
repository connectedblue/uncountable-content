<p>A very warm day at Fromebridge with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a> to continue putting up the willow screens near the reserve entrance. <p>Caroline and I donned helmets, grabbed the tape measure and spirit level and started to bash in the first post.</p>



<p>We started the pilot hole with the wrecking bar, but it soon became clear after a few sessions with the rammer that the posts were not really sinking further than 400mm (they need to be 800mm).</p>

<p>We thought that maybe we had just hit a particularly tight spot, so marked out the next post to see if it was any easier.  But despite even better preparation with the starter hole, they just weren&#8217;t shifting any deeper.</p>



<p>It seems the ground has just compacted and hardened further with the lack of rain.  We didn&#8217;t encounter this difficulty last time, and we may need to dig and concrete in the posts next week if it doesn&#8217;t rain hard before then.</p>



<p>Rene and Louise fixed the panels in place to the existing posts and they looked really good and did the screening job that was intended.   As a temporary measure, we just tacked the remaining screens into the partially sunken posts ready to finish next week.</p>



<p>I took a walk with Pete to look at other parts of the reserve.  There are some fences that need re-doing and the vegetation around the oil pipeline needs continued management.</p>



<p>We visited the laid hedge at the eastern boundary and were impressed by how well it&#8217;s started to bud and sprout already.  It&#8217;ll be thick with cover before the season is out.</p>
