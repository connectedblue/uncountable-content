<p class="wp-block-paragraph">We took no more than about 50cm each side which was a mixture of thistle and other plants.  We then disposed of the arisings in a couple of discreet spots where they will rot down.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">After tea break, we decided to head to the species rich grassland and control the thistle coming up there.  It&#8217;s been off this year with the grazing &#8211; although they have had full access the whole summer to the field, it has hardly been touched.  As a result it looks the best we&#8217;ve seen it so far, and there were plenty of bird&#8217;s foot trefoil, red clover and other species.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">While the rest of the team worked on removing the thistle, I took the brush cutter and cut again around the oil pipeline.  I think I&#8217;ve done that 3 times in the last few weeks as we keep everything short to discourage water voles from returning before engineering work is due to commence.</p>
