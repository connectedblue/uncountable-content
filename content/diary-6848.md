The weather forecast was pretty grim and it was already drizzling heavily as we headed down the valley towards the site.</p>

<p>Right by the woodland edge was a very large tree covered with bramble at the base.  Chris and I spent fifteen minutes cutting through with the brushcutter which enabled David to then fell the tree.</p>



<p>Meanwhile further back, some of the smaller trees were being taken out.  We created what has to be the number one dead hedge we&#8217;ve ever done as a group across the middle of the copse.</p>



<p>The large tree took quite some felling, and was finally down just before break.  Quite a bit of cross cutting was then required before we could drag to another dead hedge further down.</p>



<p>By the end of the session, the transformation was amazing &#8211; around 300 square meters of slope opened up where the grassland can now thrive.</p>



<p>In late afternoon I attended a meeting of the <a href="https://www.creategloucestershire.co.uk/cam-dursley-creatives">Cam &amp; Dursley Creatives</a> where I was pitching for funding to use at the <a href="https://www.facebook.com/dursleycodeclub">Dursley Code Club</a>.  There were no objections so hopefully that will arrive next week.</p>
