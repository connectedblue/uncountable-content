<p>A stunning new location with <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a> at a SSSI looking across the Windrush valley.  From this vantage point, we could see two sites where we have previously <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/02/rolling-with-gorse/" data-type="post" data-id="4243">bashed gorse</a> and <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2024/11/bank-along-the-windrush/" data-type="post" data-id="3937">restored grassland banks</a> over the last year.  </p>



<p>Add to this further fields we have surveyed, harvested and sown, a huge amount of project work was laid on display.</p>

<p>The easiest way to remove large chunks of gorse is to cut the main stem as close to the ground as possible.  David created some large glades with the chainsaw in two intervals of the scrub line which had a dual purpose &#8211; firstly grass was exposed and secondly brash piles could be laid at the end.</p>



<p>After the cut material was stacked, Tom could then brushcut the very long sward along the opened up ride.  This was then raked off to remove nutrient.</p>



<p>All in all, it fitted together very nicely &#8211; there were horizontal and vertical rides that connected together, cut and raked and ready for grazing later in the year.</p>
