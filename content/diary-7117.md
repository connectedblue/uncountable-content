<p>It&#8217;s been a fun few months helping with the construction of a part of the new <a href="https://www.theengine.org.uk/the-escape-room-dursley">Dursley Escape Room</a> game for when it opens at the <a href="https://www.chantrycentre.org/">Chantry Centre</a> in a week or two.  I&#8217;ve been working with students at Rednock who designed the puzzles and wrote the initial code, and also with Pete who helped with the hardware design.</p>



<p>So today was the day to finally deliver and get it installed.  This would be the last time I&#8217;d be able to easily plug it into my PC, so I spent a final morning making surely that everything was working as robustly as possible.  </p>



<p>After lunch, I took the kit down to the new escape room, where Mark and Adrian were doing some decoration.  We decided it would be best to just install it straight away, so there is one less thing to worry about getting ready for the launch.</p>



<p>The biggest challenge was the solid brick wall.  It was insanely difficult to drill into and destroyed more than one drill bit.  We also struggled getting the holes marked cleanly onto the wall.  But between the three of us, we got over the hurdles and I was able to set up the puzzle to give it&#8217;s first live test. </p>



<p>Shortly after, the students from Rednock arrived to carry on with decoration.  When I left, the box was being painted green, so can&#8217;t wait to see it next time when the room is completely finished.</p>



<p>This project was cooked up in a <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/09/coffee-code-and-riverfly/" data-type="post" data-id="6427">coffee with Charlie last September</a>, and it was great to see it finally get delivered.  The whole escape room relocation has been a great community effort, and will be a fantastic asset for the town for many years to come.</p>
