<p class="wp-block-paragraph">So today was the day for the <a href="https://offbeatcollective.org.uk/">Offbeat Dursley</a> festival to come to town.  I got up very early and made sure I was down at the market place well before 8am as we had a lot of people arriving to set up.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The weather forecast was decidedly better that this time yesterday, but we still had a strong prevailing wind whipping through the eastern end of the market place from Silver Street.  So my role for the first hour was to erect the green and white wind break panels to create a sheltered area for the stalls.  They could be tied with rope, but a good supply of cable ties was also a necessity to stop them flying away as the wind created a giant sail.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The walks were due to kick off at 9.45.  We had 3 planned &#8211; a bee walk, heritage walk and a herb walk.  All three had 15-20 people attending which was remarkable for that time of day.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">When they returned at 11am, the <a href="https://www.dursleymalechoir.org.uk/">Dursley Male Voice Choir</a> was striking up and sang for 25 minutes facing parsonage street.  This attracted a really good crowd and it was nice to see children playing on the pavement in front of the choir.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Just as they finished their last song, the first drops if rain appeared so they disassembled quickly and everyone took shelter under the market place.  This created a huge buzz of activity arounf the stalls which didn&#8217;t really let up all day.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There were talks and workshops taking place upstairs and craft activities over in the Methodist hall opposite.  It all seemed to go like clockwork and the steady flow if visitors carried on right through early afternoon.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">At 3pm, we started to disassemble the stalls while the final Story time was taking place upstairs.  This enabled the Fish Disco to start at 3.30pm which was the grand finale to the day.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">There were so many people there in costume, both adults and children and the whole market place was buzzing with dancing and music.  It was really quite the spectacle.</p>
</div></div>

</div></div>


<p class="wp-block-paragraph">By 5pm, the disco dancers were flagging and the last of the decorations could be removed.  We left the green panels up because the Hoptician were taking over holding an evening event.  By 5.30pm we were done and it was back home to chill out.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The biggest shout out goes to Georgie White Winter who conceived and organised the whole event.  I have no idea how she managed to curate and produce such a fantastic event.  I hope there will be more Offbeat events to follow in the future.</p>
