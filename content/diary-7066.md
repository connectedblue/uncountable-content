<p>Water levels have dropped considerably recently, so no worries setting out early to meet Tony for the regular monthly check.</p>



<p>We were able to get to the usual parts of the river today, especially around the middle so we hoped for a good sample.  And it didn&#8217;t disappoint &#8211; the usual large number of cased caddis and mayfly were supplemented by blue winged olives and a handful of ordinary olives.</p>



<p>Still not a huge number of gammarus, but certainly more than the pitiful numbers we&#8217;ve seen here recently.  We could quite decide whether it was 90 or 110, so we erred on the side of caution and recorded 90 (that&#8217;s a score of 2 rather than 3 &#8211; it didn&#8217;t really justify a three).</p>



<p>Overall, a score of 8 which was very good indeed for this time of year.</p>



<p>In the afternoon, I headed over to the library to meet the Rednock students and finalise the escape room game.  They had some good final suggestions which I&#8217;ll fold into the game, before building the traffic light with Pete tomorrow.</p>
