<p>Two different activities today about controlling electric lights.  I met with Pete at the <a href="https://www.chantrycentre.org/">Chantry Centre</a> to figure out a design for a programmable traffic light for the escape room. </p>



<p>The basic idea is to have a tablet where you enter codes and when all the puzzles have been completed, the traffic light goes from red to green and the players can leave the game.</p>



<p>We had already sketched out a basic design over the phone, but it&#8217;s useful seeing the physical space and we made some refinements to how it will work.  Just need to order the parts list and we can test the whole thing.</p>



<p>Straight afterwards I headed over to the library for this week&#8217;s <a href="https://www.facebook.com/dursleycodeclub">Dursley Code Club</a>.  The Rednock students making the app that will drive the traffic light attended and they made excellent progress on debugging the app.</p>



<p>Another student brought in their pico board connected to some lights and laptop.  He made some sample programs in python that could switch the lights on and off in different sequences.  It was a nice demonstration of hardware and software working together and something I&#8217;d like to do more formally at the club.</p>
