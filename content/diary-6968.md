There were two major tasks today &#8211; raking the large area of lower grassland that had been flailed by the farmer and burning the gorse piles we had excavated last time from the upper slopes.</p>

<p>After tea break I joined the fire team.  It&#8217;s very unusual for us to burn &#8211; I only remember doing this once before in the last seven years with Glorious Grasslands.  But in this case there were old and new brash piles right in the middle of the grass slopes, so it made sense to burn.  As it&#8217;s an SSSI, prior permission was sought to do this.</p>



<p>David had brush cut a two meter firebreak around the fire as a safety measure.  But we found as it gained strength, the wind wafted burning ash and set fire to patches in the vicinity.  We had to be on the ball to keep it contained within the fire site.</p>



<p>Once it had established though, we quickly chomped through the big piles through to lunch.  I then left as I had an appointment with BBC radio later in the afternoon.</p>



<p>Tomorrow I&#8217;ll be on the BBC Gloucestershire breakfast show talking about Dursley, so this afternoon&#8217;s call was with the producer to go through the background they need to prepare and also record some &#8220;teaser&#8221; snippets they can use beforehand.</p>



<p>Everything pretty straightforward, but an early start tomorrow as I have to be in the studio in Gloucester by 7am.</p>
