<p>The hawthorn was quite unusual.  Not overly high, very thick stems and a vertical tangle of branches.</p>



<p>It&#8217;s deer country, so they were probably continually browsed from above while growing out thickly sideways.</p>



<p>The best technique for clearance was snipping and sawing most of the branches, leaving a stumpy stem for Tom to cut with the chainsaw.</p>



<p>We struggled to find somewhere suitable for the many arisings.  We tried a couple of places in the open, but these were all covering grassland so far from ideal.</p>

<p>In the end, we settled on removing everything to the bottom of the slope near the fence.  Even if the landowner doesn&#8217;t remove them, the grass down there was less species rich than the upper slope.</p>



<p>In the late afternoon I drove directly to the Cam &amp; Dursley Creatives meeting, still in my outdoor gear.  Was very well attended and a lot of business to cover. </p>
