<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Normally today would have just been me and Tony doing our regular monthly riverfly check at Ebley Meadows.  But three new people had joined our group and wanted to have an introduction to what we do as a group.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">So, in the end it was a mini taster session.  We actually have two official locations in the same stretch, so I took one with Imogen and Tony did the second spot with the others.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">It looked pretty promising on first bucket glance.  Lots of gammarus which have been sadly lacking across all the sites recently.  But we managed to also find a good number of other species and recorded a score of 7, which was pretty respectable for the area.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">In the first part of the afternoon, i went to the Chantry Centre for the steering group of a project to identify all the events in the area for older people.  This is a hugely positive and important project, and my role is to curate and present them digitally within the <a href="https://discoverdursley.org.uk/">Discover Dursley</a> platform.  The initial data gathering has been a huge success, so I can start the long job of loading the details in.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">He had written a youtube clone which he&#8217;d put live on a free server and given logins to his friends.  It was stunning in it&#8217;s completeness.  You could upload videos, play and comment on them, and receive notifications.  It&#8217;s hard to overstate just quite how creative this is for a 12 year old, especially the deployment itself.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I did have a quick talk about some security implications of putting demos like this live, and how you might serve out video in a more scaleable way. I&#8217;m pretty sure he&#8217;ll be deploying whole suites of apps over the next year or two.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I&#8217;m always blown away by what I witness at code club.  The creativity of this generation is something to behold, and I&#8217;ll never tire of running it.</p>
