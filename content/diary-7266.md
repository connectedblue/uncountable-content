<p class="wp-block-paragraph">The monthly Fromebridge orchard work party this morning with the <a href="https://www.gloucestershirewildlifetrust.co.uk/volunteer">Gloucestershire Wildlife Trust</a>, and we had a similar job to do with creeping thistle as <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2026/05/thistle-control-at-fromebridge/" data-type="post" data-id="7203">we&#8217;ve done in the main reserve</a> recently.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I first went into Pockett&#8217;s Orchard in 2021 and it was mostly filled with scrub above head height.  Since then, with the management and grazing it has transformed into a grassland, although major pockets of bramble, thistle and dock keep popping up during the height of the growing season.</p>

<p class="wp-block-paragraph">Instead I chose areas which had a lot of thistle and dock, but also plentiful grass growing in between.  This should offer the biggest bang per buck, because the cattle are still in this area and will aftermath graze the wilted thistle and knock the grass back.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">I broke the back of it before coffee break, where we had Caroline&#8217;s delicious cake and caught up on some orchard news with Andy.  After that, everyone moved towards the bottom of the orchard and it finished up looking pretty clear by the end of the session.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">On the way back to the car park, I swung by the small meadow at the end of the reserve to take a look at the corky-fruited water drop wort.  The nice thing about that field is we have spread green hay to the adjoining field in the past and it definitely showed the benefit this year as the corky fruited had spread quite a bit into the main grassland.</p>



<p class="wp-block-paragraph">It was a pleasant morning weather-wise.  I didn&#8217;t think it was too hot at the time, but I did get a sunburnt neck which I felt stinging later in the day.</p>
