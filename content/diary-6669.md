<p>After a short while, we could start adding the bigger logs that had accumulated and soon the fire was packing a lot of core heat.  That gave us a chance to start unpicking an old log pile that was tangled in bramble.  We managed to get about half of that freed, but there&#8217;s still an awful lot of material still to cut out in a future work party.</p>



<p>There was a pile of willow bed material from a <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/09/de-willowing-the-reed-bed/" data-type="post" data-id="6435">clearance session at the ocean</a> back in September which needed to be burned.  We cleared most of it, but when moving the final two stumps, there was a family of 6-8 field voles.  So we left them in place.  It shows that you can&#8217;t really leave a habitat pile in the wrong place for too long, because something will always move in.</p>



<p>After lunch, we didn&#8217;t add any more fuel onto the fire, so headed back to join the others and help clear the cut brash from the old hedge line.  They had planted some of the new trees, and the new hedge is starting to take shape.</p>
