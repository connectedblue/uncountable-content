<p>The task today was to create a stile to allow maintenance access into a fruit tree pen within the species rich grassland.  The pen is completely surrounded by stock proof barb wire fencing which is difficult to duck under.</p>



<p>It took a little while at first to sort out the parts, sizes and methodology for the build.</p>

<p>The core design was to have two fence posts next to each other on each side of the bar and a cross piece of step screwed on top.  But we had to figure out how much of the post would be underground, how to bang them in and also how to cut pointed spikes into the shorter posts.</p>



<p>The first post was way too long, took ages to sink into the ground, and was still too high a level. So we measured a sensible step height and took a saw to fix it. Fed up a bit with the slow progress, we took a tea break and enjoyed delicious cake from Caroline and Clare.</p>



<p>After the break we had a lot more focus.  The next three posts went in very quickly.  We found that the sledge hammer used on it&#8217;s side was much more effective that the upside down post rammer.  We also had to put a piece of scrap wood to protect the top of the post from the hammer blows.</p>



<p>During the last part of the construction the sky went very dark grey and it absolutely bucketed down for about 20 minutes.  This slowed down progress a little bit as every movement just became more effort.  </p>



<p>But we finally got the last screw in and able to climb out of the pen on the new stile.  The sun then came out blazingly giving no hint of the chaos of the last hour.</p>



<p>The rest of the team had planted pear trees and other whips, as well as knocking back bramble from the fence boundary.  All in all an excellent afternoon&#8217;s work.</p>
