<p>Woke up to another pleasant and sunny April morning and decided to go for a two hour walk to stretch the legs and enjoy the wild flowers out at the moment.</p>



<p>The bluebells seemed to come out early this year and by now they are carpeted everywhere in the woods.  The wild garlic is also holding its own, so most of the beech woods had a mixture of both.</p>

<p>On the way back, I stopped by Uley Common to check on progress.  I was rather pleased to see a handful of dog violets at the northern end &#8211; I don&#8217;t recall seeing them last year.</p>



<p>And the primroses have continued to spread well, along with the celandine.  Not seen an orchid yet, but I&#8217;m hopefully there&#8217;ll be one or two in flower over the next couple of weeks.</p>

<p>In the afternoon, it was off to the <a href="https://www.chantrycentre.org/">Chantry Centre</a> for the inaugural steering group meeting for a new community project focused on events for older people.  This is something that will be hosted on <a href="https://discoverdursley.org.uk/">Discover Dursley</a> down the line, so it&#8217;s good to be involved in the formation of the requirements and scope.</p>



<p>There were a good cross section of representation from different groups and the perspectives were varied as a result.  This will be a good project to be involved with over the coming months.</p>



<p>Almost straight after, the students arrived from Rednock for the escape room session so that was a really good chance to review the app functionality with Charlie and Adrian for the first time.  The gameplay is pretty much there, but we agreed some final tweaks that will improve things.</p>



<p>I had also sent Adrian a design for the enclosure around the traffic light hardware.  He&#8217;ll attempt to put this together this week, which will enable Pete and myself to assemble the inner workings.</p>



<p>So all in all, a very productive day.  In the evening, I purchased the domain for <code>digitaldursley.uk</code> which is a new project we will formally try and get off the ground in the coming months. </p>
