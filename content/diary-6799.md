This is a large site with significant areas being managed for biodiversity including species rich grassland. I was last here a <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2024/01/sloping-off-the-green/" data-type="post" data-id="2859">couple of years ago</a>, so it&#8217;s good to see progress on previous management.</p>

<p>The area was previously quite dense scrub and  bramble we needed to be very careful of hidden stumps in the grass.  There was a lot of mullein and oxeye daisy which had taken from the green hay, so very encouraging.  I worked from bottom to top, which is my preferred method on a steep slope.</p>



<p>We got the whole area done by lunch, and were joined by others to help get the area raked off properly.  There seemed to be a lot of species after the grass was raked away, especially toward the top.</p>



<p>In the evening, I had a zoom call with the <a href="https://offbeatcollective.org.uk/">Offbeat Dursley</a> team where we made quite good progress on planning for the event in June.</p>
