<p>Last <a href="https://www.cotswolds-nl.org.uk/looking-after/our-grasslands-projects/glorious-cotswolds-grasslands/">Glorious Grasslands</a> work party of the year saw us return to a site we last visited <a href="https://diary.uncountable.uk/2025/03/steeped-in-scrub/" data-type="post" data-id="4349">back in March</a>. We&#8217;ve worked on it for many years and you can really see the difference.</p>



<p>The first part of the morning was on the steep slope that we first cleared a few years ago.  The grassland is very species rich &#8211; even at this time of year you could see lots of herbaceous plants packed between the grasses.  But the previously cut hawthorn stumps were sprouting stress shoots about a foot high.  So we made our way down the slope, cutting them back to ground level.</p>

<p>At the top of the slope, David had been working on a semi circular glade, which really opened up the grassland and a route up to the top.  Removing these dense tree canopies will allow light to get through and the grassland to spread across the slope.</p>



<p>We finished at 1.30pm and made our way up to <a href="https://www.theoldprison.co.uk/">The Old Prison</a>  for our Christmas meal.      It was good to see Jane there, who&#8217;s recovering well from an operation. We also gave her a birdbox with camera to celebrate her recent 80th birthday.</p>



<p>The food was varied and delicious with plenty of good conversation.  A good end to the year. </p>
